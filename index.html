We develop, design and build a subsea ROV. To do this we utilize the knowledge acquired during the studies and put them into practice under supervision from our partners at NTNU and the industry.

Who are we?

Vortex NTNU is a student organisation that designs and builds underwater robots- also knowns as remotely operated vehicles or ROVs - each year to participate in the MATE ROV international student competition. We are headquartered at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology in Trondheim, Norway. The team is composed of 6 students from a wide variety of faculties that are passionate about science and technology. For example the team has mechanical, electrical, material science and cybernetics engineers, consisting of master students.

How did it start?

This project started at NTNU spring 2015 through subject matter experts in teams. Pioneers, which consisted of Daniel Tran, Morten Leeland, Geir Kulia, Kjetil Hope and Helge Fylkesnes wanted more practical experience.

What makes us special?

In cooperation with Norsk Elektro Optikk we aim to deploy their LaserShark equipment to depict the seabed by projecting a laser line and process the image frame to find the exact position of the laser line. This is used to make a 3D model of the seabed.
